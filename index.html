    Executive Summary:

    The primary motivation for Intel Semiconductor Education Program at Central State University (ISEP-CSU) is to create a pipeline that produces underrepresented students for prepared for Intel careers by giving students knowledge and practical experience in semiconductor fabrication techniques, processes, and equipment. The primary strategy to achieve this goal is to establish a semiconductor educational program at Central State University (CSU), the only public historically black university in the state of Ohio, with the support of our collaborating partners, including Columbus State Community College, Stark State Community College, The Ohio State University, and Wright State University. The ISEP-CSU project addresses three key topics: i.) curriculum development, ii.) laboratory equipment upgrades, and iii.) student experiential opportunities. The project’s objectives are to 1.) Develop a minor program in computer hardware technology and a certificate program in semiconductor processing by establishing new courses related to semiconductor manufacturing in the Department of Manufacturing Engineering and Department of Mathematics and Computer Science, and 2.) Establish an undergraduate Microfabrication Lab to provide experiential learning opportunities for underrepresented undergraduate STEM students. Lab work about applied engineering projects enables practical skills learning needed for semiconductor manufacturing in fabrication and processing, operations and designs, system optimization using experimental design, Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Machine Learning, Data Analytics, and Visualization. The partnering institutions, Wright State University and the Ohio State University will assist CSU in designing and developing the new courses, establishing the Microfabrication Lab, and training CSU and community colleges faculty and students in semiconductor manufacturing. As a result, we expect to produce 50 certificate holders and 50 associate degree graduates in the second and third years of the project, with plans for sustainability in place after grant funding expiration.


    Objectives:

    — Developing a certificate program in Semiconductor Processing,
    — Developing a minor program in Computer Hardware Technology,
    — Establishing an undergraduate Microfabrication Lab at CSU,
    — Summer scholarships for undergraduate & graduate STEM students.
